Welcome to Clean Campus



Waste management is one of the most difficult challenges faced by institutions and Grama panchayats, Municipalities in Kerala. The problem becomes severe as non-degradable polymer (plastics and rubbers) waste gets mixed up with the bio-waste. As a result, the plastic, rubber waste delays or sometimes even arrest the degradation of the bio-waste. Also, this makes the recycling of plastic waste quite impractical. Hence it is essential to separate them before disposal.Everyone needs to manage the waste generated in their home and workplaces. So it is important that we need to manage the waste generated in our campus before advising others. So we have launched the Clean Campus Programme, a mission to ensure cleanliness in our campus.

Clean Campus Programme was inaugurated in connection with the Awareness Seminar “Waste Management: Begin at Our Home” held on 16.06.2016 in connection with the World Environment Day. The mission is to ensure cleanliness in our campus. The seminar was sponsored by the Kerala State Council for Science Technology and Environment and Department of Science and Technology, Govt. of India

Action
Plan

• We have arranged differently coloured waste bins to put bio-waste, plastic waste, recyclable paper waste, glass waste and metal waste with the financial support of the PTA of our college.

Organic
Wastes

• We have already implemented the biogas plant in the college canteen. The biowaste collected from the campus will be put in this biogas plant daily.

E-Waste

• In order to manage the e-waste generated from the campus, we have a tie-up with the Clean Kerala Company, Thiruvananthapuram. They will collect tube lights, toner cartridges, CFLs and CDs also apart from e-waste.

Recycling

• The plastic waste will be sent for recycling. The recyclable paper collected from the campus will be sold as per office rules.
